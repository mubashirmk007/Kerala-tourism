Thiruvananthapuram

Called the ‘Evergreen city of India’, Thiruvananthapuram is the capital of the state of Kerala.

Kollam

In Southern Kerala's attractive port town of Kollam, called the 'cashew capital of the country'.

Pathanamthitta

It was carved out of Idukki, Alappuzha and Kollam districts. The district is close to the western Ghats.

Kottayam

Kottayam's natural beauty and radiance are underscored with hardearned credentials, and it is a thriving commercial hub.

Alappuzha

Alappuzha's beautiful Backwater, wondreous waterways and gorgeous canal walkways truly make it "The Venice of the East"!

Idukki

The majestic beauty of the Western Ghats and the lush green tea plantation adds to the charm of Idukki.

Ernakulam

Ernakulam refers to the eastern, mainland portion of the twin cities of Ernakulam-Kochi in central Kerala, India.

Thrissur

riginally known as Thirusivapperoor and previously by its anglicized form Trichur, Thrissur is the cultural capital of Kerala.

Palakkad

Palakkad is often referred to as the granary of Kerala. The mesmerizing sight of the exotic Palmyra trees.

Kozhikode

Kozhikode is known to the world as the Center of Malabar, the land of spices and festivals.

Malappuram

Malapuram is a city that has seen an extensive history in the past and has developed through it.

Wayanad

With its green highlands, lush valleys and cool clime, Wayanad is one of the most beautiful retreats of Kerala.

kasargode

Kasaragod has enchanting beauty and is a land of historical importance. The district is home to the most fascinating forts.

Kannur

Kannur is most renowned for the Theyyam performances in its temples. This magnificent art form plays an integral part in the culture.
